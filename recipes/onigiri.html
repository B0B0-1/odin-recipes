Onigiri

Description

This is my take on onigiri with no filling. It's basic and a good place to start learning trying making these delicious rice balls. From this recipe, you can branch out into tons of different fillings and combination. These are fairly cheap and fast to make, and molding the rice can be really fun and satisfying.

This recipe is a mix of multiple I've tried over the last few years. I cobbled together the parts that worked for me, and now I have this recipe that my family loves. This recipe will only make a few onigiri, but you can easily scale it up for more. Also, I use an Instant Pot to cook the rice, but you can cook it in your prefered method. Give it a shot and see how you like them.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sushi rice
  • 1 jar of furikake seasoning
  • 1 pack of sushi nori
  • 1/4 cup rice wine vinegar
  • 1 tbs sugar
  • 1 tsp salt

Directions

  1. Wash rice three times
  2. Add 1/1 ratio pf rice to water into Instant Pot
  3. Cook on high pressure for 11 minutes, and wait 10 minutes after to release pressure
  4. Mix rice wine vinegar, sugar, and salt in a microwave safe bowl
  5. Microwave seasoning mixtrure in 30 second intervals, and stil until sugar and salt are disolved
  6. Cut nori to strips around 1 inch by 6 inches
  7. Empty furakake on a plate
  8. Get a large baking pan or cutting board to spread rice on to cool
  9. Get another large plate or pan to put your finished onigiri on
  10. Fill a bowl with water
  11. Set all your preparations up in an area you can easily reach them all
  12. Put cooked rice on your large pan and pour seasoning over it
  13. Gently fold rice and seasoning together using a spatula
  14. Once rice is cool enough to handle, wet your hands in the water bowl to prevent sticking and shape a hand full of rice into a small triangle
  15. Take your newly formed onigiri and roll its sides in the Furakake so it sticks to the outer edge
  16. Choose a side to be the bottom of your onigiri, place a strip of nori evenly from one side to the other, around the bottom of the onigiri
  17. Set aside your completed onigiri
  18. Repeat until you run out of rice
